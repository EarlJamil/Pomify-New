Pomodoro Technique

The Pomodoro Technique improves focus by breaking study time into intervals (Pomodoros) separated by short breaks.

  • Study for 25 minutes straight.
  • Take a 5-minute break.
  • After 4 Pomodoros, take a longer 15–30 minute break.
  • Reduces burnout and boosts consistency.

Eisenhower Matrix

This method helps prioritize tasks by urgency and importance using four categories:

  • Do First: Urgent & Important
  • Schedule: Important but Not Urgent
  • Delegate: Urgent but Not Important
  • Eliminate: Neither Urgent nor Important

Great for managing time effectively and reducing procrastination.

Mind Mapping

Mind mapping organizes ideas visually, connecting concepts around a central topic.

  • Start with a central idea in the middle.
  • Add branches for related topics.
  • Use colors, icons, and keywords for better recall.
  • Ideal for brainstorming and complex subjects.
